Computer Science Student & Software Developer

I'm Aaron Paddy, a Computer Science major at Grambling State University with a passion for building intelligent systems and scalable software. My experiences span both academic research and hands-on engineering, where I've worked on projects at the intersection of AI/ML, software systems, and real-world applications.

As a Machine Learning Research Assistant at Grambling State, I developed GAN-based models to simulate and evaluate hardware security systems, contributing to resilience discussions in cryptographic design. Previously, as a Software Research Intern at Utah State's DIRECT Lab, I built a real-time EV charging simulation framework, optimizing concurrency, scheduling, and network performance for dynamic wireless charging in urban traffic.

Beyond research, I love turning ideas into working software. My projects include building a Slack MCP server that integrates AI agents into Slack workspaces, a face-recognition attendance system, and an AI-powered scheduling platform that helps students automatically generate course schedules.

I'm actively expanding my expertise across full-stack development, distributed systems, and applied AI. I enjoy solving complex problems, collaborating across teams, and building technology that makes systems more efficient, intelligent, and accessible.
